01

What to watch this semester

Read this even if you read nothing else. Each paper on its own is manageable — the hard part is where all three land on the same days. These are the pressure points, in the order they arrive.

Peak crunch · early Nov

Three finals in three days

All three exams fall in the same short window — three days in a row. Plan your revision backwards from these dates; the exam week itself is too full to learn anything new.

Wed 4 Nov · INP (1230–1540)  →  Thu 5 Nov · HS2 (1230–1640)  →  Fri 6 Nov · Pharmacology
The good news

The three exams don't overlap

Tight as the window is, no two exams share a day: INP on Wednesday, HS2 on Thursday, Pharmacology on Friday. You can sit and revise for them one at a time, not all at once.

Wed 4 · INP  ·  Thu 5 · HS2  ·  Fri 6 · Pharmacology
October gauntlet

Coursework + practicals + a test, all at once

The month before exams is the busiest stretch: the big Pharmacology essay, the roster-based clinical practicals, and HS2's last test all fall together, then roll straight into exam week.

Mon 12 Oct Pharm essay · 5–30 Oct Vital Signs & Med-Admin window · Mon 26 Oct HS2 Test 3 closes
Early spike · late Aug

Your first two hurdles arrive together

Orientation week feels quiet, so this is easy to miss — a graded test and a must-pass drug-maths hurdle fall two days apart in the same week.

Sun 23 Aug · HS2 Test 1 closes  ·  Tue 25 Aug · Med Calc Test
Mid-sem break

The "break" isn't fully a break

Two INP Case Study stream due-dates fall inside the mid-semester break, and HS2 Test 2 closes on the Monday it starts. If either is your stream, you're working through it.

Mon 21 Sep · Test 2 closes + Case Study (2B)  ·  Mon 28 Sep · Case Study (1A)
After the exams

Placement runs Nov → Dec

Once finals finish you move into ~116 clinical hours across inpatient & community settings, right through to mid-December, with three competency sign-offs.

Mon 9 Nov – Fri 18 Dec · sign-offs 20 Nov, 4 Dec, 18 Dec
Play it smart

Playing the semester well

The other side of those pressure points — a few simple moves that make this semester easier, based on where the work piles up. Take what's useful.

Week 1

Clear the admin traps first

Two small things gate the whole semester: the Lab H&S quiz needs 100% before you can enter any lab, and lab-group swaps close Fri 31 Jul. Both take minutes now and are painful later.

Front-load

Move the Pharm essay early

It's 50% of the paper and due Mon 12 Oct — right when the practicals and Test 3 hit. Drafting it in the quieter Aug–Sept period takes one big task out of October.

Prep once

Treat the October practicals as one block

INP Vital Signs and Pharm Med-Admin share the same 5–30 Oct window and skill set. Prepare the practical once and you're ready for both hurdles.

All semester

Keep working Booklet A all semester

HS2's un-starred cases feed the tests and the starred* ones feed the exam. A little each week beats any single cram before the exam.

Test windows

Use the ProctorU week deliberately

Each HS2 test is open Mon–Sun. Sit it early once the material's taught, or hold to Friday for the full syllabus — just decide on purpose rather than drifting into Sunday night.

Early Nov

Plan backwards from the exam cluster

With three finals in three days (Wed 4 → Thu 5 → Fri 6 Nov), the revision has to be done before that week — the days in between are for light review, not learning things for the first time.

Introduction to Pharmacology

722 · Kaiako: Joan Mackie (part-time) · Coordinator TBA

Pharmacological concepts & safe medication practice

150 hrs total
60 taught · 90 self-directed

The shape of it

Two graded pieces (essay + exam, 50/50) sitting on top of two must-pass practical hurdles. The theory is drug classes — mechanisms, therapeutic uses and side effects — plus safe administration and patient medication education. Coordinator was still listed as TBA in the export, so watch for that appointment. What you'll cover

Pharmacological principles Safe medication administration Pharmacokinetics & dynamics Medication calculations Elderly, pregnancy & polypharmacy Immunology, vaccines & allergy Legal & ethical standards Licit & illicit drugs, toxicology

  • Live drug references you'll actually use: NZ Formulary4 and Medsafe.5
  • Potter & Perry Ch 19 for medication-safety fundamentals.3

Recommended readings & references

The texts, official NZ nursing documents, and drug references behind each paper. Numbers match the superscripts in the sections above.

1Patton's Anatomy & Physiology (ANZ). HS2's core text, via ClinicalKey Student; chapters mapped to each weekly topic (CVS Ch 31–36 + 44, lymphatic 43, neuro 18–22, endocrine 25–26, reproduction/genetics 45–48, special senses 24). The backbone for every test and the exam.
2Knights, K., Rowland, A., Darroch, S., & Bushell, M. (2023). Pharmacology for Health Professionals (6th ed.). Elsevier. — The set text for Pharmacology; on ClinicalKey.
3Frotjold, A., & Bloomfield, J. (2026). Ch 19: Ensuring medication safety. In Potter & Perry's Fundamentals of Nursing: ANZ edition (7th ed., pp. 477–511). Elsevier. — Most-referenced chapter for safe administration.
4New Zealand Formulary. The national prescribing reference — dosing, interactions, cautions. nzformulary.org
5Medsafe — Medicine Information. NZ regulator; data sheets & consumer medicine information. medsafe.govt.nz
6NZNO. (2024). Guidelines for Nurses on the Administration of Medicines. — Professional standard underpinning the med-admin competency.
7Nursing Council of NZ. (2025). Te Tiriti o Waitangi, Māori Health & Kawa Whakaruruhau / Cultural Safety: Guidance for Nursing Education and Practice. — Core context for INP's cultural-safety and Te Tiriti outcomes. nursingcouncil.org.nz
8Nursing Council of NZ. (2025). Scope of Practice & Standards of Competence for Registered Nurses. — The NCNZ competencies your placement sign-offs assess against.
9Nursing Council of NZ. Code of Conduct for Nurses. — Referenced across the "Professionalism in Nursing" threads in both HS2 and INP.
10Healthify — He Puna Waiora. Plain-language NZ health & medicines info, useful for patient-education tasks. healthify.nz
11Pharmac — Most-Prescribed Medicines. Which drugs you'll actually meet in NZ practice — helpful framing for the pharmacology essay. pharmac.govt.nz
12MIT Guide to APA 7th Referencing (2024) & the Nursing/Health APA examples. Required style for all written work; check Turnitin similarity before final submission.
13HS2 Case Study Booklet A (Canvas). The core resource running through Health Science 2 — un-starred cases feed the tests, starred (*) cases feed the exam. Repeatedly named the most important exam-prep resource; work it over the whole semester.
14pepeha.nz. Build a mihi/pepeha for the INP "Meet & Greet" introduction. pepeha.nz/create-pepeha

Sources. Synthesised from the Canvas export of all three Semester 2 2026 papers (Health Science 2 · 722.541, Introduction to Nursing Practice · 722.556, Introduction to Pharmacology). Weightings, formats and dates are quoted from the syllabus, assessment-overview, calendar and timetable pages; weekdays were cross-checked against the 2026 calendar.

Much of Canvas was still gated at export time. Dates — especially stream-specific practicals, roster times, and the early-November exams — may shift; confirm against the live Canvas calendar as content unlocks. This is a study aid, not the official course document.
