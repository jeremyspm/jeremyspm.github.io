Health Science 2

722.541 · Principal Lecturer: Hannetjie Meintjes

Anatomy & physiology for safe practice

3 tests = 50%
Exam = 50%
Avg 50% to pass · no sub-minimum

The shape of it

Rigidly structured: 3 modules · 3 topics each · ~4 weeks each · 2 labs each. A test closes at the end of every module; the exam is Week 13. The whole thing is built around Case Study Booklet A — un-starred cases are tested in the tests, starred (*) cases are tested in the exam. That booklet is repeatedly called the single most important exam-prep resource.13

Module 1 · Wk 1–4

Cardiovascular Respiratory Lymphatic Blood vessels & heart Cardiac physiology / ECG Blood-pressure regulation Gas exchange & transport Acid–base balance Fluid & electrolytes

Modules 2 & 3 · Wk 5–11

Musculoskeletal Nervous Endocrine Bones, joints, levers Muscle action & energy Brain & spinal cord Glucose & calcium homeostasis Reproduction Special senses Genetics

Useful to know

  • Tests are sat online through ProctorU, with a window that runs Monday–Sunday of test week. Past students who sat early scored well — you can also wait until Friday if you want all content taught first.
  • The exam is on campus (MIT rules require ≥50% of a course assessed on campus); the three tests are the online half.
  • Anatomy Mondays pre-teach each topic before lectures — used well, they turn the fast pace into an advantage. Core reading is Patton's Anatomy & Physiology via ClinicalKey, mapped chapter-by-chapter each week.1
  • Lecturer's own advice for the pace: attend every lecture & lab, do the formative quizzes as each system is taught, and form a study group early for exam-prep scenarios.

Introduction to Nursing Practice

722.556 · Coordinator: Janine Craig · Lecturer: Lisa Andrew

Philosophy & principles of practice + first placement

300 hrs · 30 credits
116 clinical hrs

The shape of it

The "how to be a nurse" paper — the nursing process, cultural safety and Te Tiriti o Waitangi, determinants of health, assessment skills and communication, threaded to the NCNZ competencies. It's the paper with your first clinical placement, so a chunk of it is hands-on and pass/fail rather than percentage-graded.

Topics — theory

Social determinants of health Demography & epidemiology Community needs assessment Tikanga & cultural safety The nursing process Informed consent Holistic & pain assessment Nursing care plans Evidence-based practice Health literacy

Topics — applied

Vital signs Family-centred care Therapeutic use of self Long-term conditions Diabetes mellitus Infection prevention & control Rheumatic fever Discharge planning Clinical documentation Primary health care

Useful to know

  • Your stream (1A / 1B / 2A / 2B) sets your dates for the case study, CLS simulations, and practical assessments — check the roster early; the timeline above shows the range, not your personal date.
  • The Community Profile is a group presentation briefed back in Week 2 — start forming your group and picking a community well before September.
  • Written work uses APA 7th and goes through Turnitin.12 The "Meet & Greet" asks for a pepeha — build one at pepeha.nz.14
  • Placement (weeks 16–21, 9 Nov – 18 Dec) is inpatient + community; the Standards of Competence sign-offs are non-negotiable gateways, not scored marks.

Introduction to Pharmacology

722 · Kaiako: Joan Mackie (part-time) · Coordinator TBA

Pharmacological concepts & safe medication practice

150 hrs total
60 taught · 90 self-directed

The shape of it

Two graded pieces (essay + exam, 50/50) sitting on top of two must-pass practical hurdles. The theory is drug classes — their mechanisms, therapeutic uses and side effects — plus safe administration and patient medication education. Coordinator was still listed as TBA in the export, so watch for that appointment.

What you'll cover

Pharmacological principles Safe medication administration Mechanisms of action Therapeutic uses & side effects Drug classes for nursing Medication calculations Medication education & health literacy Legal & ethical standards

Core resources

  • Knights et al. — Pharmacology for Health Professionals (6th ed) is the set text.2
  • Live drug references you'll actually use: NZ Formulary4 and Medsafe.5
  • Potter & Perry Ch 19 for medication-safety fundamentals.3

Assessments

Useful to know

  • The Medication Calculations Test is a drug-maths hurdle in late August — because re-sits are offered, it's easy to underestimate, but you can't pass the paper without clearing it. Practise calculations early.
  • The essay is worth half the paper and is due before the exam crush (12 Oct) — get it done in the quieter first half of the semester.
  • Med-Admin competence runs concurrently with INP's Vital Signs assessment in October — the two papers share that practical window, so prep once, use twice.

Recommended readings & references

Footnotes from the brief above — the texts, official NZ nursing documents, and drug references that give the context behind each paper. Numbers match the superscripts in the sections.

1Patton's Anatomy & Physiology (ANZ). HS2's core text, accessed via ClinicalKey Student; chapters are mapped to each weekly topic (e.g. cardiovascular Ch 31–36, musculoskeletal Ch 11–17, neuro Ch 18–22, endocrine Ch 25–26, reproduction/genetics Ch 45–48). The backbone for every test and the exam.
2Knights, K., Rowland, A., Darroch, S., & Bushell, M. (2023). Pharmacology for Health Professionals (6th ed.). Elsevier. — The set text for Pharmacology; on ClinicalKey.
3Frotjold, A., & Bloomfield, J. (2026). Ch 19: Ensuring medication safety. In Potter & Perry's Fundamentals of Nursing: ANZ edition (7th ed., pp. 477–511). Elsevier. — Most-referenced chapter for safe administration.
4New Zealand Formulary. The national prescribing reference — dosing, interactions, cautions. nzformulary.org
5Medsafe — Medicine Information. NZ regulator; data sheets & consumer medicine information. medsafe.govt.nz
6NZNO. (2024). Guidelines for Nurses on the Administration of Medicines. — Professional standard underpinning the med-admin competency.
7Nursing Council of NZ. (2025). Te Tiriti o Waitangi, Māori Health & Kawa Whakaruruhau / Cultural Safety: Guidance for Nursing Education and Practice. — Core context for INP's cultural-safety and Te Tiriti outcomes. nursingcouncil.org.nz
8Nursing Council of NZ. (2025). Scope of Practice & Standards of Competence for Registered Nurses. — The NCNZ competencies your placement sign-offs assess against.
9Nursing Council of NZ. Code of Conduct for Nurses. — Referenced across the "Professionalism in Nursing" modules in both HS2 and INP.
10Healthify — He Puna Waiora. Plain-language NZ health & medicines info, useful for patient-education tasks. healthify.nz
11Pharmac — Most-Prescribed Medicines. Which drugs you'll actually meet in NZ practice — helpful framing for the pharmacology essay. pharmac.govt.nz
12MIT Guide to APA 7th Referencing (2024) & the Nursing/Health APA examples. Required style for all written work; check Turnitin similarity before final submission.
13HS2 Case Study Booklet A (Canvas). The through-line of Health Science 2 — un-starred cases feed the tests, starred (*) cases feed the exam. Repeatedly named the most important exam-prep resource; work it over the whole semester.
14pepeha.nz. Build a mihi/pepeha for the INP "Meet & Greet" introduction. pepeha.nz/create-pepeha
